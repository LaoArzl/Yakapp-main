Kau – answers the question “who?”
Kew – used in second person or addressing the person you are taking to. A clue on how to use this word is it is never used in a beginning of a sentence.

“Pitu kew” – come here
“Sine kew?” – who are you?
“Ngagad kew” – wait
“Intag kew tuli?” – where are going to sleep?

Nun – this is used in second person and a singular form

“Ine alen nun? – what is your name?
“Sine amma nun?” – who is your father?
“Intag luma nun?” – where is your house?
“Sine sawe nun? – who’s with you?

Bin – bin on the other hand is used in second person and plurar form

“Ine alen bin? – what is your name?
“Sine amma bin?” – who is your father?
“Intag luma bin?” – where is your house?
“Sine sawe bin? – who’s with you?

Bin

“Intag luma bin? – where is your house?
Since luma is a property of someone, you can use bin after the object.

In
In is used closely like bin but without the possession of anyone.

Examples:
“Intag hospital-in?
“Where is the hospital?”
It can be also used when the you’re using a verb in its past tense.

Examples:
“Aku mag tilew-in”
“Who asked?”

“Sine mag-aka hin?”
“Who said it?”
